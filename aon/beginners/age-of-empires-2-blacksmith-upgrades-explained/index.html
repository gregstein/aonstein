Age of Empires 2 Blacksmith Upgrades Explained

GregStein June 7, 2019 Beginners 2 Comments
Please Share

The blacksmith is a must use building in Age of Empires 2 due to upgrades being a game changing factor only if you know which upgrades have priority over others. In this guide, we will be looking at which upgrades you should be getting first and learn more about upgrading your unique units because some of them are tricky.

Blacksmith upgrades are divided into 2 categories, Attack and Armor upgrades as shown in the table below:

Tip: Fletching, Bodkin Arrow, and Bracer also provide additional range for units and buildings.

What Blacksmith Upgrades Should you Research First?

Archery Unit are favored with a priority to Attack Upgrades e.g Fletching, Bodkin and Bracer () are more important than any Armor upgrades.

Why? Non-upgraded Archery units initially have less attack and are usually created in large numbers thus attack upgrades will double their damage input however armor upgrades have secondary priority. Attack upgrades for archery units also provide an additional range giving these units an offensive opportunity from further distances.

Cavalry Unit are favored with a priority to Armor Upgrades e.g. Scale Barding, Chain Barding and Plate Barding () are more important than any Attack upgrades.

Why? Cavalry units tank a lot of damage from arrows thus it makes more sense to upgrade armors first so they tank even more damage.

Infantry Unit are favored with a priority to Armor Upgrades e.g. Scale Mail, Chain Mail, and Plate Mail () are more crucial than any attack upgrades.

Why? Infantry units are initially weaker against archers and upgraded cavalry that is why upgrading armors should increase their resistance therefore more damage is dealt.

 

Now these are all the upgrades available at the blacksmith for every Age

Feudal Age Blacksmith Upgrades

I have purposefully added military buildings next to every upgrade because an upgrade affects units of an entire building or two buildings the case of “Forging”.

In other words,

 Fletching affects all Archery Range units except Hand Cannonner

 Padded Archer Armor affects all Archery Range units including  Hand Cannonner

Forging affects all Barracks & Stable units

Scale Mail Armor affects all Barracks units

Scale Barding Armor affects all Stable units

 

Castle Age Blacksmith Upgrades

Once you research feudal age upgrades, new upgrades will be unlocked in the same spot making it easier for you to keep upgrading the same unit.

Let me walk you into it again

  Bodkin Arrow affects all Archery Range units except Hand Cannonner

 Leather Archer Armor affects all Archery Range units including Hand Cannonner

Iron Casting affects all Barracks & Stable units

Chain Mail Armor affects all Barracks units

Chain Barding Armor affects all Stable units

 

Imperial Age Blacksmith Upgrades

Finally, Imperial Age unlocks even more upgrades and it is worth mentioning that “Blast Furnace” exclusively provide +2 attack.

Yet another walk through

  Bracer affects all Archery Range units except Hand Cannonner

 Ring Archer Armor affects all Archery Range units including Hand Cannonner

Blast Furnace affects all Barracks & Stable units

Plate Mail Armor affects all Barracks units

Plate Barding Armor affects all Stable units

 

Excellent! So far we have learnt that blacksmith upgrades affect 3 basic buildings: Archery Range, Barracks, and Stable.

Let’s move on and have a quick look at blacksmith upgrades for UU (Unique Units).

Are Blacksmith Upgrades Good Enough?

At Feudal Age, the Blacksmith upgrades are only good enough for infantry and Archery Units. Cavalry Units on the other hand are better off with [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Bloodlines” content=”+20 Hit Points” close=”yes”]Bloodlines[/su_tooltip] () before considering the blacksmith.

At Castle Age, the Blacksmith upgrades are yet important for infantry and Archery Units. Later on and as far as Archery Units are concerned, It’s highly recommended to consider the following:

An Archery Unit is divided into two types: Mounted Archery Unit, Foot Archery Unit and [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Ballistics” content=”Fire Accuracy for moving Units” close=”yes”]Ballistics[/su_tooltip] () is a must research as soon as possible because It makes it possible to take down moving units.

Mounted Archery Unit benefits from [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Bloodlines” content=”+20 Hit Points” close=”yes”]Bloodlines[/su_tooltip] (), [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Husbandry” content=”10% Movement Speed” close=”yes”]Husbandry[/su_tooltip] (), [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Thumb Ring” content=”100% Accuracy Firing” close=”yes”]Thumb Ring[/su_tooltip] (), [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Parthian Tactics” content=”+1 Melee Armor, +2 Pierce Armor and +2 attack bonus against Spear-line” close=”yes”]Parthian Tactics[/su_tooltip] () and [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Chemistry” content=”+1 Attack” close=”yes”]Chemistry[/su_tooltip] () 

Foot Archery Unit benefits from [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Thumb Ring” content=”100% Accuracy Firing” close=”yes”]Thumb Ring[/su_tooltip] () and [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Chemistry” content=”+1 Attack” close=”yes”]Chemistry[/su_tooltip] () 

 

A Cavalry Unit benefits also from [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Bloodlines” content=”+20 Hit Points” close=”yes”]Bloodlines[/su_tooltip] () and [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Husbandry” content=”10% Movement Speed” close=”yes”]Husbandry[/su_tooltip] ()

An Infantry Unit benefits also from [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Squires” content=”10% Movement Speed” close=”yes”]Squires[/su_tooltip] (), [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Tracking” content=”+2 line of sight” close=”yes”]Tracking[/su_tooltip] () and [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Arson” content=”+2 attack against buildings” close=”yes”]Arson[/su_tooltip] ()

A Naval Unit benefits also from [su_tooltip position=”north” shadow=”yes” rounded=”yes” title=”Ballistics” content=”Fire Accuracy for moving Units” close=”yes”]Ballistics[/su_tooltip] (), Careening () and Dry Dock ().
